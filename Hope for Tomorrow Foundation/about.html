About Us

Our History

Established in 2018, the Hope for Tomorrow Foundation is a registered Section 18A non-profit organization dedicated to supporting underprivileged children's education in the townships and rural areas of Gauteng. The organization was created to address post-apartheid educational inequality.

The foundation focuses on three main initiatives:

  • Digital literacy workshops to equip learners with essential technology skills.
  • Scholarships for Grades 10–12 to help students access further education opportunities.
  • After-school tutoring in Afrikaans, English, and math to strengthen foundational learning.

Since its inception, the foundation has partnered with 15 schools, recruited 150 volunteer tutors ranging from college students to retired teachers, and assisted over 2,500 children across Soweto, Alexandra, Katlehong, and surrounding communities.

Mission

To provide underprivileged youth with resources, education, and mentorship that end cycles of poverty and pave the way for postsecondary education and employment.

Vision

A South Africa where all children have equal access to education, irrespective of their background.

Target Audience

Our programs and initiatives are designed for learners, parents (including those who do not speak English), volunteers, corporate sponsors (aligned with B-BBEE regulations), donors, and partner schools.
